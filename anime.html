Sagas

The most recognizable and well-liked Shonen series of all time, Dragon Ball Z has spawned a large multimedia brand that includes manga, anime, movies, video games, and much more. The story centers on a cast of protagonists with amazing power who defend the universe and their home planet against extraterrestrials, lethal automatons, and other entities. It is the series responsible for popularizing the cliché of power levels. Throughout its many sagas, each one had formidable antagonists that the protagonists had to defeat.

Saiyan saga

The series throws the earth's defenders a curveball right away in this saga by making them prepare for an extraterrestrial onslaught without their best fighter, Goku. Vegeta, a beloved supporting figure, makes his debut.

Namek saga

Known for one of the most drawn-out fights in all of anime, Goku and allies travel to Namek to assemble the Namekian Dragon Balls and resurrect his comrades who were struck down during the Saiyan Saga. This is also the first appearance of the Super Saiyan form.

Cell saga

When a time-traveling warrior warns the Z Warriors of an oncoming android disaster, they must get ready for everything while learning that a bigger threat is still hidden. Any surviving Saiyans are now able to transform into Super Saiyans and beyond during this arc, which also provides room for character growth for a variety of other characters. Additionally, Trunks, Vegeta's kid, makes his debut here, and the SSJ2 form also makes its debut.

Majin Buu saga

In this saga the tale advances many years forward with a new adversary named Majin Buu on the horizon in this last arc of the original anime. The Saiyan transformations are explored in much more detail in this story, particularly through fusions and the debut of the SSJ3 form.
